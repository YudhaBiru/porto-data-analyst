US Stock Market and Financial Indicators Analysis (Composite scoring method)

Project Summary

The project involves analyzing 2018 US stock market data for the Biru Firm to assist a client with long-term investment decisions. The analysis evaluates a dataset of 4,391 stocks across 11 sectors, utilizing 223 financial indicators to identify top-performing investment opportunities based on fundamental company health and performance metrics. The primary objective is to identify the top 5 stocks for long-term investment and understand the characteristics of the top 5 performing sectors.

The project aims to use composite scoring to rank stocks and leading sector performers based on profitability, growth, valuation, and financial health indicators. A composite scoring methodology was applied, assigning 25% weight each to D/E Ratio, P/E Ratio, Revenue Growth, and ROE, using both global and sector-specific peer ranking methods.

Scope Of Work / Achievement

- Cleaned a dataset of 4,391 stocks down to 696 relevant entries by removing outliers and null values.

- Developed a composite scoring model weighted at 25% per metric to rank stocks against industry peer performance.

- Identified the top 5 performing sectors and specific stocks like ESNT and REGN for long-term investment strategy.

- Analyzed 223 financial indicators to provide actionable recommendations for portfolio diversification across 11 different market sectors.
